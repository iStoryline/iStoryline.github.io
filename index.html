iStoryline is composed of three UI components. (a) shows the panels that controls the visual properties of storylines. (b) is the visualization canvas. (c) presents easy-to-use interactions which enable users to lay out storylines according to their design preference.

Visual Encodings

  1. Lines represent characters.
  2. Time-axis is placed horizontally.
  3. The vertical grouping of lines indicates when and who are together.

Procedural

In this phase, we ask students to follow these basic visual encodings of storylines, freely choose stories, and manually draw storylines. Students are allowed to use pen with any color and paper with any materials. After they finish the drafts, we first summarize some new features, and then conduct an interview with all students. By doing so, we can understand the design space of hand-drawn storylines and validate the design metric of state-of-the-art techniques. More conclusions can be found in the paper. Selected assignments can be accessible via the gallery link.
